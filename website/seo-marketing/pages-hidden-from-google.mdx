**Where to find this**: Dashboard → Website Builder → Settings (gear icon) → Sitemap Builder → View/Edit
</Info>

## What this means

When Google shows **"No information is available for this page"** beneath your website link in search results, it means Google has found your site but cannot crawl or read the page content.

The most common cause in CoachIQ: **all pages are set to hidden in the Sitemap Builder.**

<Frame>
  ![Sitemap Builder with all pages hidden](/images/website-sitemap-all-pages-hidden.png)
</Frame>

When every page shows the crossed-out eye icon (as shown above), Google treats your entire site as uncrawlable. No meta descriptions, titles, or content will appear in search results until pages are made visible.

## How to fix it

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open Website Settings">
    In the Website Builder, click the **Settings** gear icon in the left sidebar.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Open Sitemap Builder">
    Scroll to **Sitemap Builder** and click **View/Edit**. A modal opens showing all your pages.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Unhide your pages">
    Review each page. If the eye icon is crossed out, the page is hidden from Google.

    Click the **eye icon** on each page you want Google to index. The icon will change to a solid eye, meaning it is now visible and crawlable.

    Your core pages — **Home, About, Programs, Pricing, and Contact** — should almost always be visible. Only hide pages like thank-you confirmations, draft pages, or private content.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Publish">
    Close the modal and click **Publish** to update your sitemap.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## After unhiding your pages

Once your pages are visible in the sitemap, submit your sitemap to Google Search Console so Google re-crawls your site faster. Google typically picks up changes within a few days to two weeks.

See: [Get Your CoachIQ Website Indexed on Google](/website/seo-marketing/seo-google-search-console)

<Note>
Unhiding your pages in the Sitemap Builder does not make previously private pages public — it only tells Google it is allowed to crawl them. Anyone with a direct link could always visit those pages regardless of sitemap visibility.
</Note>
