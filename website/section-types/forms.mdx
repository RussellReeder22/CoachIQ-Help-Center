### **How to Use the Form Section**

#### **Step 1: Add a Subheading**

- Navigate to your form section.
- Add a subheading by clicking on **Add Subheading**. This helps organize your form and provides context to users.

#### **Step 2: Select or Create a Form**

- **To select a form**, click on `Select Form`. Choose from the list of previously created forms.
- **To create a new form**, ensure you first save and publish your current site to avoid losing any edits. Then, click `Create New Form`.

#### **Step 3: Save and Publish**

- Click on **Website Form** to populate the form on your site.
- Once your form is set, click `Publish` to make the changes live.
- You can now view your live site to see the form in action.

> **Note:** Always save your site before creating a new form to prevent any data loss from recent edits.

### **Tips and Best Practices**

- **Organize Forms with Clear Subheadings:** Use subheadings to categorize forms, making them intuitive for users to navigate.
- **Regularly Save and Publish:** Frequently save your progress to avoid data loss and ensure your site is up-to-date.
- **Test Forms Before Going Live:** Preview your forms to ensure functionality and user-friendliness.

### **Frequently Asked Questions**

**Q: How can I ensure my changes are saved before creating a new form?** A: Always click `Save` and `Publish` before initiating the creation of a new form. This process secures your recent edits.

**Q: What should I do if my form does not appear on the live site?** A: Double-check that you have published your changes. If issues persist, contact support for assistance.
