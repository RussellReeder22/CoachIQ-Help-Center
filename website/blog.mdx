Your blog lives inside the Website Builder as its own section, separate from your regular website pages. Each post you create appears automatically in your blog archive.

<Info>
**Where to find this:** Website Builder → page dropdown → **Add New Page** (to create the blog page) or the book icon in the left sidebar (to manage posts)
</Info>

## Set up your blog page

<Steps>
  <Step title="Create a new Blog page">
    In the Website Builder, click the page dropdown at the top and select **Add New Page**. Title the page **Blog**, then click **Create Page**.

    CoachIQ will generate an automatic template for the page. Click **Publish** to save it.

    <Note>
    The blog editor is separate from your main website pages. Everything you create inside it is a blog post — not a standard website page.
    </Note>
  </Step>

  <Step title="Set your Blog Archive Page">
    Click the book icon in the left sidebar to enter the blog editor.

    <Frame>
      ![Book icon highlighted in the Website Builder left sidebar](/images/blog-section.png)
    </Frame>

    You'll see a **Blog Archive Page** setting with a page selector dropdown. Select the **Blog** page you just created.

    <Frame>
      ![Blog Archive Page selector showing a dropdown with website pages listed](/images/blog-archive-page.png)
    </Frame>
  </Step>

  <Step title="Add the Blog Post Archive section">
    Go back to your Blog page in the Website Builder. Delete the default CTA section, then click **+ Add Section** and select **Blog Post Archive** from the Default category.

    Click **Publish**. This is the page that displays all your posts to visitors.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Add the blog to your site navigation">
    To make the blog discoverable, add it to your header navigation, footer, or both. You can also add a **Default Blog Posts** section to any page (such as your homepage) by clicking **+ Add Section** and selecting **Default Blog Posts** — this will automatically display your latest posts wherever you place it.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Create blog posts

<Steps>
  <Step title="Add a new post">
    Inside the blog editor, click **Add New Post**. Enter a title for your post and click **Create**. CoachIQ will open an automatic template you can build on.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Add your content and publish">
    Add sections, text, images, and any other content to the post. When you're ready, click **Publish**. The post will appear in your blog archive automatically.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Reuse a post layout (optional)">
    To keep a consistent visual style across posts, click **Add New Post**, give it a title, and click **Create**. Remove all the default sections, then click **+ Add Section**, navigate to a previous post, and copy the sections over. This lets you match the layout of an existing post without rebuilding it from scratch.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Add featured images to posts

Featured images appear in the blog archive alongside each post title. Once you have a few images set, the archive displays in a three-column grid.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open Post Settings for each post">
    In the blog editor, open the post you want to update. Click **Post Settings** (typically in the top toolbar or sidebar).
  </Step>

  <Step title="Upload a featured image">
    Under **Featured Images**, upload the image you want to represent this post. Click **Publish**.

    Repeat for each post. Once images are set, the blog archive will display them in a three-column layout. Visitors can click **Read More** to open the full post.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Common questions

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Why is the blog separate from my other pages?">
    The blog editor manages posts as individual content pieces that feed into the archive automatically. Regular pages (like About or Services) are managed separately because they have a fixed place in your site structure, while blog posts are meant to grow over time and be listed together.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="How do visitors find my blog?">
    Add the Blog page to your header navigation, footer navigation, or both. You can also embed a **Default Blog Posts** section on any page (like your homepage) so recent posts are always visible without visitors having to navigate away.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Can I use the same visual layout for every post?">
    Yes. Create your first post with the layout you want, then for each new post: click **Add New Post**, remove the default sections, click **+ Add Section**, select your existing post, and copy the sections over.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Do blog posts help with SEO?">
    Yes. Blog posts are one of the most effective ways to rank for local search terms. Writing about local sports events, school teams, area tournaments, and seasonal training topics attracts parents searching in your area. Each post is its own indexed page.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>
